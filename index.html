Home

We are a breed of hardware freaks. We have our own e-magazine, lectures and discussions. We have our own lab to try out new things by ourselves, and our weapons include devilious Cathode Ray Oscilloscopes, Function Generators to name a few. Managed by Prof. Sanjeev Gupta and driven by the student community, this is the ultimate geek paradise. This is where you will find people soldering circuits, wiring motors and working on projects like communicating with motor through the serial port of your computer, or making a new multimedia keyboard, or creating disco lights for the next dance night, or something as elegant as hosting a server on a Raspbery Pi, this is where it all happens. This club organizes lectures on different topics of current interest, provides introductory lab sessions on them, organizes debates, quizzes etc. and hold contests like the popular DLD competition, student paper and idea presentations, CRO gaming contests, or our trademark crossword puzzles and even basic robot building contests.

About

Electronic Hobby Center is a student run organization of Dhirubhai Ambani Institute of Information and Communication Technology (DA-IICT). The centre is meant to provide the students a platform where they can unleash their creative skills and talents especially related to electronics without any boundations. The ideology of the centre is be the best in Electronics ""Towards Electro Excellence"". The defination of democracy i.e.for the students, by the students and of the students holds good for the centre. This hobby center initiated by Prof. Suryakant Ingle (Former Professor, DA-IICT) and Bhupesh Batra (B. Tech 2001) facilitates designing, testing and fabrication of various electrical, electronic and electromechanical gadgets/devices. Students not only gain practical methods of learning but also get a platform to try out their creative skills. It initiates them into art of making electronic things with their own hands and furthermore helps them in their final year project. The center organizes various technical and non-technical events from time to time like Technical Paper Presentation, Technical Debates, Robotics, CRO Revolution, Circuit building and trouble shooting and Educational cum sight seeing tours. It also organizes various Labs, Lectures and Doubt solving sessions on different topics from time to time. Apart from above mentioned technical aspects, EHC also help students in building team spirit by encouraging them to work together. The organization motivates capable students to learn technique of decision-making and managing things and develop all-round ability by making them part of many committees within itself which are meant for overall coordination and organization of different activities and events throughout the year. We always welcome suggestions for improvement. Prof. Incharge: Prof. Sanjeev Gupta
